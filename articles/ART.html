Creating your own programming language is now easy!

Ever wanted to create your own programming language?
Ever looked at some of the documentation for popular programming languages?
If not, go look at the Java specification. No really do it, here is a link: https://docs.oracle.com/javase/specs/.
No-one out there, including Java developers, know the specification of its own language, for example, how many operator priority levels are there? No one really knows.
This is where ART comes in. Developed by Adrian Johnstone and tested by Elizabeth Scott: https://github.com/AJohnstone2007/ART , ART (ambiguity retained translation), allows you to write concise and formally verifiable programming languages.
As the specification is very concise, it is easy for other programmers in your team to use and adapt your language.
